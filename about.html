Brief History of the Mind Masters Liberia Initiative

The Mind Masters Liberia Initiative (MMLI) was founded on June 10, 2025 by B. Hallison F. Thomas, a passionate Liberian mathematics student and youth academic leader. The vision behind the organization was born out of a deep desire to revitalize academic excellence in Liberia—particularly in the fields of mathematics, science, and intellectual competitions.

While serving as the Assistant Secretary General for the Mathematics Department at the University of Liberia Student Association, Mr. Thomas observed a concerning trend: many talented students in critical thinking and mathematics were left unsupported, underexposed, and under-celebrated. Inspired by this gap and by the success of academic initiatives across Africa, he established MMLI as a national platform to discover, develop, and spotlight academic talents across the country.

Since its inception, the organization has worked to bridge the gap between talent and opportunity by:

  • Hosting high-level academic competitions
  • Creating mentorship and training programs
  • Building a community that values knowledge, analytical skills, and innovation

The initiative’s flagship project, the National Mathematics High School Championship, was launched in 2025 to identify and elevate top-performing students in mathematics from schools across Liberia. This marks the beginning of a new wave of academic empowerment for Liberia’s youth.

Mind Masters Liberia Initiative is not just an organization—it is a non-profit movement dedicated to making academic excellence attractive, competitive, and celebrated.
